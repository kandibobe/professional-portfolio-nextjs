# Data Breach Incident Response Plan

This document outlines the procedure to be followed in the event of a data breach at **[Your Name] Photography**, in accordance with GDPR requirements.

## 1. Definition of a Data Breach

A personal data breach is a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to, personal data.

## 2. Immediate Response (First 24 Hours)

1.  **Identify and Contain:** Determine the source and extent of the breach. Immediately take steps to stop the breach (e.g., change passwords, disconnect affected systems).
2.  **Assessment:** Identify what data was affected and the potential risk to individuals.
3.  **Documentation:** Log the time, date, and details of the discovery and the containment steps taken.

## 3. Notification Phase (First 72 Hours)

1.  **Notification to Authority:** Under GDPR, if the breach is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, it must be reported to the relevant Data Protection Authority (e.g., Garante Privacy in Italy) within **72 hours**.
2.  **Notification to Individuals:** If the breach is likely to result in a _high risk_ to the rights and freedoms of individuals, they must be notified without undue delay.

## 4. Evaluation and Long-Term Action

1.  **Post-Incident Review:** Analyze the cause of the breach and implement measures to prevent recurrence.
2.  **Update Security Measures:** Refine technical and organizational measures (e.g., better encryption, stricter access control).
3.  **Record Keeping:** Maintain a permanent record of the breach, its effects, and the actions taken.
